Sprint 5 Journal:

What surprised me the most about Human Skills learning is....
I was most surprised how powerful this content can be. When its applied to my learning environment it has quite an impact.

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?
1. I have learned that I am not as good at problem solving as I had originally thought. When struggling to learn something, I quickly place blame on external factors.
2. When I am frustrated, the answers to questions get harder to find.
3. I can apply what I've learned in "Human Skills" to make problem solving much smoother.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?
These roles are important because they can help to ground yourself. When grounded its easier to see thing for what they are. They can create a deeper situational awareness. This can be especially helpful when working with colleagues.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?
How there is great stuff to be learned but how easily it is all forgotten and how quickly I revert back to my normal self.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?
Exploring my identity/values and how to put that down on paper.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?
To create well rounded graduates who will be able to function in a working with others type environment.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time?
Yes and no.

Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?
I have no aspirations to either, get a job programming or to work with others so I would prefer to spend this time learning more about programming.
