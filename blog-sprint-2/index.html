Ethical Decision

I'm a builder and for a period of time I contracted myself out to a particular company. I had started my apprenticeship with this company so it was nice to go back later on in my career and work within a familiar environment. The owner of the company was a fun guy to be around and considered him a friend. Unfortunately, he also had a toxic personality. After being let down by him time and time again, I eventually had to make the decision to cut ties and never work with him again. Working for this company offered me a stable and reliable source of work when required. In the end I had to uphold my values and leave the toxic environment and sacrifice the stable income. When I reflect on it now, I am proud of that decision. I do however wish I had done it sooner.

Values & Identity

I was raised in a "figure it out for yourself" culture which I guess has led to my continuous self learning. This has a downside, not actively asking for help has became my default. Though I really value the idea of asking for help, this can be a struggle sometimes. After getting married, my biggest influence is my wife. Being is a psychologist, she has taught me great deal about how humans tick. This has really shaped my interactions with others and made me more empathetic.

Strengths

My biggest strength is an eagerness so learn. This will make it easier when encountering problems, problems equal more learnings.

Limitations

The biggest limitation I believe I have is slow processing. It takes me a while to process and understand information. This is compounded by likely having mild dyslexia. It can make it hard to keep up with others in a learning environment.

Example Of Working With Others

I was prepping a floor slab to be poured with another contactor on a building site. The other contractor was a bit older and considered himself to be the one in charge. Neither him nor I were "in charge" as we were working together toward the same goal. However, I was the person with the most experience doing this particular task. I must have portrayed this through my body language and communication because he became quite upset when I pointed out some of his work wasn't done as per plan. To reduce the tension I tried to state simply, "it cannot be done that way". In hindsight this was a poor approach. It just made him more angry and his work even worse. After a while he left me to it and I essentially started from scratch. If I were to approach this in a better way, I could have asked what his reasoning was for doing the work that way. If I took the time to understand his way of thinking, he may have been more open dialog about the problem. If we were able to chat the problem through, maybe we could have ended up with an outcome that we were both happy with.
